Bracket House

Address: S. 5th Avenue & S. 2nd Street
Year Built: 1877
Builder: William Brackett
Architect:
Owner: Cornell College
Use: guest house
Physical Description:
Style-Colonial Revival, after the remodeling in 1914-1919
Construction Type-Rectangular plan with 2 large two-story rectangular porches at each end, Two-story, wood frame

Features:

¨ Symmetrical about all facades
¨ Gable roof
¨ Horizontal clapboard siding with large full height (Two-story) relief wood pilasters between porches and main portion of house and at outside corners
¨ Small capital
¨ Casement windows (French doors) with four-paned transom above
¨ Opening onto wrought iron balconies
¨ Symmetrical windows
¨ 2 casements above main entry
¨ Main entrance has classical porch
¨ Architrave, frieze, denticulated cornice, and large round columns
¨ Door is flaked by multipainted sidelites with transom above
¨ Wrought iron balconies ornate with boat shape pattern
¨ Porches with combination (alum) windows
¨ Clapboard siding between pilasters except 2nd story of long dimension which an ornate rail with vertical balusters and diamond pattern infill in three locations
¨ Door is in the center of each porch
¨ Ornament-lattice work encloses space under porches (has now been filled in and sided)
¨ Large brick chimney on south
¨ Steps out towards top
¨ Diamond infill has been filled in and sided
¨ New louvered windows have been added to all porches except for the west porch on the 1st floor

History/Comments:

The widow of the builder, Clara, stayed in the house. She married Armstrong Spear, a Philadelphia banker who commuted from Philadelphia to Mt. Vernon. The 1914-1919 remodeling "updated" a fairly traditional 19th century house form to conform with the ten-popular Colonial Revival style.
