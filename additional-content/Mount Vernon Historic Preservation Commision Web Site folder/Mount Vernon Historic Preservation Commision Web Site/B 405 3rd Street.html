405 South 3rd Street West

· Age: 1876

· Brick Pattern: Common Flemish

· Architectural Style: Gothic. The high sloping roofs with the tower and the ornamentation are sure indicators of the Gothic period.

· Historical Significance: This home was designed by Cass Chapman and built by Marsden Keyes and William Brackett. These two builders are responsible for many of the homes built at this time in Mount Vernon: the Platner House (the music practice house), Rood House, Harlan House, and Collin House, all now belonging to Cornell College.
